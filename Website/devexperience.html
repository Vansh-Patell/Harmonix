Our development path had its fair share of highs and lows. While, working together as a team was great and and each of us contributed something worthwhile, there were also instances when we struggled with time management, which led to some delays in releases. Inspite of these difficulties, we gained a lot of knowledge about problem-solving, time management, and cooperation while building software.

Positives:
- Collaboration with team members
- Solving complex problems together
- Industry experience

Negatives:
- Communication issues at the early stages of the project
- Quality inconsistencies in the codebase
- Dependency on other team members to complete tasks before moving on to the next

After iteration 2, we realized our tests needed work. We didn't have enough tests for certain parts of our app, so we focused on making them better. We spent time making sure our tests covered all the important parts of our app, even the tricky bits. Thus the system test suite was developed thoruoghly to compensate for the other iteration's testing.

In one of our success criterias, we set a goal to make it easier to add new features. To do this, we improved how we tested our code. By making our code easier to test through refactoring, we succeeded in adding features like the queue and playlists more smoothly. This showed that our testing improvements helped us add new stuff faster, just like we wanted

Some features worked even better than we thought they would. For example, the search tool was faster and more accurate than we expected. It quickly found what users were looking for, making the app easier to use. Also, creating playlists turned out to be simpler and more helpful than we thought. Users liked how easy it was to organize their music. These surprises made our project even better!

Additionally, we would allocate more time for regular code reviews and feedback sessions to ensure better code quality throughout the development process. Also, prioritize documenting our code and project decisions to make it easier for other team members to get onboard. Finally, we would establish clearer guidelines for feature prioritization and task allocation to optimize our release planning and meet project deadlines more effectively by priotizing easier features first. These adjustments would change our overall development experience and project outcomes.
